## Quoted article/status captured text and structure

Author: Anatoli Kopadze (@AnatoliKopadze)
Timestamp shown: 2:14 PM · Apr 29, 2026

Title: “20 Claude Prompts that turn a $20 Subscription into a personal Assistant, Editor, Coach, and Analyst”

Lead text:

> You pay $20/month for Claude. Most people use 10% of it.
> Ask a question. Rewrite a text. Summarize a document. That's the average Claude session.
> That's not using Claude. That's wasting it.
> Claude can be your fitness coach, your language tutor, your negotiation trainer, your financial analyst, your editor, your strategist. Not approximately. Literally. The only variable is the quality of your prompt.
> These 20 prompts cover real situations in a real person's life. Take what you need. Skip what you don't. Use them as a base to build your own.

Prompt/use-case list captured from the article:

Deep research

1. Multi-Source Synthesis — combine multiple articles/papers/reports into one conflict-resolved brief with sections for core consensus, key conflicts, critical gaps, strongest claim, and actionable takeaway; rules include not summarizing each article separately, flagging source credibility, marking one-source claims as unverified, and staying under 600 words.

2. Devil's Advocate Mode — ask the model to attack an idea/plan/decision across flawed assumptions, execution risks, market/human reality, and the fatal flaw; rules explicitly forbid solutions or softened language.

3. Steelman Any Position — ask the model to build the strongest version of an opposing position, including core insight, evidence, where the user's view is weakest, and the strongest single argument.

4. Mental Model Builder — teach a topic through 3–5 connected mental models with core idea, application, example, common mistake, connections, and an end-check question.

Writing

5. Style mimic — analyze three samples of the user's writing and generate a thread/article/email/post in the same style, including rhythm, vocabulary, paragraph openings/closings, avoided structures, and a “signature move.”

6. The Brutal Editor — critique and edit a draft across cuts, weak ideas, missing content, structure, and the single biggest problem; then produce a tighter edited version.

7. One Text, Five Audiences — rewrite one idea for a beginner, domain expert, skeptic, journalist, and executive, then identify which audience is hardest to write for.

8. Bullets to Article — transform rough notes into a finished article/post/report/email while preserving the user's ideas, target length, audience, tone, and goal.

Career

9. Salary Negotiation Roleplay — simulate a hiring manager with budget/team constraints, realistic objections, pushback, and after-roleplay feedback.

10. Interview Simulator — run a realistic job interview from a pasted job description, asking one question at a time and grading each answer.

11. Resume Multiplier — tailor one resume to three job descriptions without inventing facts, changing emphasis and keyword alignment while flagging gaps.

12. Difficult Conversation Prep — map opening line, likely responses, word-for-word replies, trap to avoid, and exit for a hard conversation.

13. LinkedIn Bio Rewrite — create five positioning variants under 300 characters each and ban common resume clichés.

Daily Life

14. Weekly Meal Plan with Shopping List — build a 7-day meal plan from dietary constraints, calories/protein, cooking time, budget, and shopping/prep guide.

15. Legal Document Translator — translate a contract/lease/TOS/NDA into plain English, covering obligations, risky clauses, unusual terms, missing clauses, and questions for a lawyer/other party; notes it is not legal advice.

16. Personal Finance Analyzer — categorize real expenses, identify biggest leaks, quick wins, goal feasibility, and the single highest-impact behavior change.

17. Travel Planner — build a day-by-day itinerary with budget, travel style, booking needs, hidden gems, cost estimates, and overhyped places to avoid.

Learning

18. Feynman Tutor — teach one concept at a time with comprehension checks, alternate explanations for wrong answers, and final teach-back.

19. 30-Day Curriculum Builder — create a day-by-day learning plan with weekly phases, 80% doing/20% reading, checkpoints on days 7/14/21/30, and recovery if checkpoints fail.

20. Socratic Mode — ask one question at a time, never directly explain, guide correction through follow-up questions, and end by identifying the key insight.

Closing text:

> 20 techniques. Deep research that beats 90 minutes of reading. Writing that survives a brutal editor. Career prep that makes hard conversations feel rehearsed. A life assistant that handles the decisions that drain you. A tutor that teaches through your own reasoning.
> Every one of these is included in your $20/month.
