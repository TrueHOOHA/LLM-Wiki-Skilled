# Human-Token Mode Separation

## Definition
Human-Token Mode Separation is the workflow hypothesis that some AI-era knowledge work may improve when human-only cognition and token-heavy AI execution happen in distinct blocks, contexts, or physical spaces rather than being continuously interleaved.

## Scope
The concept covers deliberate boundaries between reading/thinking/writing and AI/Codex/agent execution; the use of time and place as mode switches; and the possibility that blending deep human attention with constant AI interaction can degrade both. It does not claim that all AI use during creative or analytical work is harmful, that the pattern is universally optimal, or that Hermes/Alpha/Beta should change behavior from a weak social source alone.

## Contrasts
- Versus [[Dynamic Context Loading]]: dynamic loading separates context inside an LLM run; human-token mode separation separates human attention and AI/tool execution across a workday or workflow.
- Versus [[Context Engineering]]: context engineering designs what the model sees; this concept designs when the human engages the model at all.
- Versus [[Agentic Workflows and Agents]]: agentic workflow design separates automated steps and control flow; this concept separates human cognitive states from AI/token-heavy execution states.
- Versus continuous copilot use: the hypothesis predicts that constant AI adjacency can blur goals and lower the quality of both human thinking and machine execution, but this has not been validated in the captured source.
