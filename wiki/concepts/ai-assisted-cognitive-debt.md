# AI-assisted Cognitive Debt

## Definition
AI-assisted Cognitive Debt is the hypothesis that immediate AI assistance can produce a polished output while leaving the human with less cognitive engagement, memory, ownership, or transferable understanding than if they had first generated, searched, reasoned, or struggled without the assistant.

## Scope
This concept covers cognitive offloading during learning tasks, especially when the task outcome looks acceptable but the human cannot recall, quote, explain, modify, or independently reproduce the work. It currently has direct evidence from essay writing in [[Your Brain on ChatGPT: Accumulation of Cognitive Debt when Using an AI Assistant for Essay Writing Task]] and adjacent coding-skill evidence from [[How AI assistance impacts the formation of coding skills]]. It does not claim that all AI use creates debt or that AI use causes brain damage.

## Contrasts
- Versus productivity: a task can be completed faster or more cleanly while still producing weaker human learning.
- Versus hallucination: cognitive debt can occur even when the generated text is fluent and scored well.
- Versus [[Learning-preserving AI Assistance]]: cognitive debt is the risk pattern; learning-preserving assistance is a design response.
- Versus [[Timing-sensitive AI Assistance]]: timing-sensitive assistance names the intervention variable of when and how help enters the loop.

## Evidence
- [[Your Brain on ChatGPT: Accumulation of Cognitive Debt when Using an AI Assistant for Essay Writing Task]] reports lower EEG connectivity, lower self-reported essay ownership, weaker quote recall, and tool-shaped linguistic homogeneity for LLM-assisted essay writers in a preliminary study.
- [[How AI assistance impacts the formation of coding skills]] provides adjacent medium evidence in coding: AI-assisted participants completed unfamiliar programming tasks but scored lower on no-AI mastery checks, especially debugging questions.
