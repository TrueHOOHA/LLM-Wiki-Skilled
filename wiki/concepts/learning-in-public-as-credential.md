# Learning in Public as Credential

## Definition
Learning in Public as Credential is the pattern where a visible trail of learning, experiments, explanations, and artifacts functions as evidence of curiosity, judgment, momentum, and field proximity before a formal role validates the person.

## Scope
This concept covers sharing research notes, field guides, technical experiments, essays, demos, maps, and other useful learning artifacts in public communities or channels where knowledgeable people can inspect and reuse them. It is especially relevant in fast-moving fields where formal credentials lag the frontier. It does not mean every private learning process should be public, that public attention equals competence, or that current implementation guidance should be taken from old field guides after the technology has moved on.

## Contrasts
- Versus [[Public Proof-of-work]]: learning-in-public emphasizes the process and trajectory; public proof-of-work emphasizes the artifact as evidence.
- Versus credentialism: it can supplement or predate credentials, but it does not automatically satisfy formal hiring, safety, or regulatory requirements.
- Versus content marketing: the strongest form helps other practitioners learn or solve a problem, rather than only broadcasting identity.
- Versus [[Learning-preserving AI Assistance]]: both concern learning, but learning-in-public is about external legibility and opportunity; learning-preserving assistance is about maintaining internal mastery while using AI.
