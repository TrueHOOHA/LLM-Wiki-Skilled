# Making Usefulness Legible

## Definition
Making Usefulness Legible is the communication and artifact-design practice of reducing the effort another person needs to see what problem you understand, what contribution you could make, and why your interest is specific rather than generic.

## Scope
This covers specific cold outreach, memos, teardowns, analyses, prototypes, essays, and concise explanations that reveal fit between a person's abilities and a recipient's actual context. It includes studying the person/company/problem before asking for attention, showing relevant evidence, and creating a small unit of signal. It does not include overproducing unwanted labor, asking the recipient to infer everything, or using false intimacy to pressure a response.

## Contrasts
- Versus generic networking: usefulness-legibility starts from the recipient's actual problem and context, not the sender's vague need.
- Versus [[Public Proof-of-work]]: proof-of-work is an artifact class; usefulness-legibility is the interpretive clarity that lets the artifact or message land.
- Versus [[Side Doors]]: side doors need legible usefulness to avoid becoming entitlement or spam.
- Versus [[Agent-Computer Interface Design]]: both value reducing interpretive burden, but one applies to human hiring/opportunity contexts and the other to agent/tool interfaces.

## Evidence
- [[How to enter side doors]] emphasizes specificity as signal: a useful message says, in effect, "I see what you are building, I understand this corner of the problem, here is evidence I can help, and here is why I care."
- Evidence is medium as practitioner communication advice; no controlled evidence is supplied for response rates or hiring outcomes.
