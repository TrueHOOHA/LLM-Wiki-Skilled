# Operator Taste Before Content Automation

## Definition
Operator taste before content automation is the claim that a founder/operator should manually create enough content to build judgment before outsourcing or automating a UGC/content engine. [[Matt Gittleson on vibecoded B2C app exit playbook]] gives the concrete heuristic "first 100 videos founder-made" and frames viral sense as the asset that later automation or delegation can amplify.

## Scope
This concept covers early-stage content production, hook/demo iteration, niche observation, and manual learning loops for founders using AI-assisted product building. It does not prove that 100 videos is the right threshold, that AI influencers or agents underperform, or that the claimed TikTok/Reels/Shorts posting rules generalize.

## Contrasts
- Contrasts with automation-first UGC, where agents, AI avatars, or hired creators are used before the operator understands the niche and format.
- Contrasts with product-only learning, where the builder improves features while neglecting the attention surface that brings users in.
- Complements [[Learning-preserving AI Assistance]]: both preserve human skill formation before delegating too much cognition or production to AI.
- Complements [[Distribution-first AI-assisted B2C App Building]], because the format-selection strategy depends on the operator recognizing why a format works.

## Evidence
- [[Matt Gittleson on vibecoded B2C app exit playbook]] claims the founder made every post, used under $100 in marketing spend, studied the niche daily, batched filming, rotated hook clips and demos, and tracked prediction skill. These are detailed but self-reported social claims without independent performance proof in the captured source.
