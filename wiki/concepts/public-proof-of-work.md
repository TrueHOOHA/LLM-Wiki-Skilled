# Public Proof-of-work

## Definition
Public Proof-of-work is a visible artifact that demonstrates how someone thinks, learns, builds, analyzes, or contributes before a formal credential or interview has to explain them.

## Scope
The concept includes essays, guides, prototypes, teardowns, datasets, tools, research notes, videos, events, communities, and other artifacts that let a relevant audience inspect the person's judgment and competence. It is strongest when it is specific to a real problem or field and useful to others. It does not guarantee opportunity, replace private diligence, prove all claimed expertise, or justify creating public artifacts that expose confidential or unsafe information.

## Contrasts
- Versus [[Making Usefulness Legible]]: public proof-of-work is one mechanism for legibility; legibility also includes targeted private notes, referrals, conversations, and direct evidence.
- Versus [[Learning in Public as Credential]]: public proof-of-work emphasizes the artifact as evidence; learning-in-public emphasizes the process of sharing learning over time.
- Versus influencer branding: the durable value is inspectable thinking and contribution, not audience size alone.
- Versus formal credentials: public artifacts can supplement credentials but may not satisfy regulated or standardized hiring gates.
