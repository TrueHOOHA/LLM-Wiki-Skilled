# Jobs as Search Problems

## Definition
Jobs as Search Problems is the frame that hiring is not only a matching process over posted roles and resumes, but a noisy search for people, problems, proof, trust, timing, and budgets that can be brought into alignment.

## Scope
This concept covers the substrate beneath job boards: organizations contain humans with bottlenecks, unscoped needs, limited attention, uncertain budgets, and imperfect ways to detect who can help. Candidates likewise search for people, missions, problems, and contexts where their skills or learnable abilities become valuable. The frame does not erase real constraints such as credentials, labor law, geography, compensation, hiring policy, or unequal access.

## Contrasts
- Versus ordinary job-board search: a search-problem frame includes hidden or not-yet-posted needs, not just visible openings.
- Versus [[Abstraction-leak Reasoning]]: jobs-as-search-problems is the career-market instance of mapping the official surface ontology to underlying mechanics.
- Versus [[Side Doors]]: this is the model of the terrain; side doors are tactics for moving through it.
- Versus personal-brand advice: the key variable is not generic visibility but signal that helps the right people solve real problems.
