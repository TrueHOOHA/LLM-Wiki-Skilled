# Anterior Mid-cingulate Cortex and Tenacity

## Definition
Anterior Mid-cingulate Cortex and Tenacity is the cautious neuroscience framing that the anterior mid-cingulate cortex (aMCC) participates in effortful goal pursuit by integrating cost, benefit, attention, affect, memory, and movement signals needed to persist through challenge. In Tolaria, this concept is supported as a plausible tenacity/perseverance mechanism, not as proof that disliked tasks uniquely grow a brain region or that the aMCC is literally "the will to live." [[Podcast Notes tweet on anterior mid-cingulate cortex and will to live]] is the weak-social source; [[aMCC Will-to-live Viral Claim Assessment]] separates it from stronger literature.

## Scope
This concept covers source-checked claims about the aMCC as a hub for effort, cost/benefit computation, tenacity, perseverance, challenge expectation, attention allocation, goal attainment, and related aging evidence. It does not cover generic motivational advice, all anterior cingulate cortex functions, or claims that any single brain region is the seat of willpower, survival instinct, or life meaning.

Supported narrower claims:
- A 2020 Cortex review argues that aMCC's network position lets it integrate signals from multiple brain systems to predict energy requirements needed for attention allocation, encoding, movement, and goal attainment.
- A 2013 Neuron report found that electrical stimulation near aMCC/cingulate network sites in two epilepsy patients produced autonomic changes plus a subjective expectation of imminent challenge and determination to overcome it.
- A 2016 Journal of Neuroscience superaging paper reported preserved/youthful thickness in salience/default-mode network regions including anterior midcingulate cortex and correlations with memory performance in older adults.

Unsupported or overstated claims:
- "Only grows when you do things you don't want to do" is too exclusive and causal for the evidence captured here.
- "Will to live region" is a viral simplification; the closest source-checked phrase is "will to persevere" from an electrical-stimulation report, not a general life-will construct.
- Superager thickness findings do not establish that hardship, disliked tasks, or Goggins-style discipline caused the observed anatomy.
