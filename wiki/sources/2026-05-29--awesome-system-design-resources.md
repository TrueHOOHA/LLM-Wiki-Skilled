# Awesome System Design Resources

## Summary
`ashishps1/awesome-system-design-resources` is a high-star GitHub repository that curates free system-design learning and interview-prep resources. The repository is primary evidence for its own structure, popularity, outbound-link map, and AlgoMaster funnel, but only secondary evidence for system-design pedagogy and weak-to-medium evidence for individual linked engineering claims until those links are ingested source by source. Its durable Tolaria value is a source map: it exposes a broad [[System Design Learning Taxonomy]], a large practice-problem inventory, and one concrete [[System Design Interview Answering Framework|seven-phase answering framework]], while the 142 outbound links should remain candidates for targeted future ingestion rather than being bulk-promoted from this card.

## Key Claims
1. The repository positions itself as a free system-design learning and interview-prep index and points readers toward AlgoMaster articles, courses, newsletters, YouTube channels, engineering articles, and distributed-systems papers.
2. Its topical taxonomy covers foundations: core concepts, networking, APIs, databases, caching, asynchronous communication, distributed systems/microservices, architectural patterns, and system-design tradeoffs.
3. It includes 45 system-design interview practice prompts split by easy, medium, and hard difficulty; many prompts are YouTube links or AlgoMaster pages rather than self-contained problem statements in the README.
4. The strongest method-like artifact in the capture is AlgoMaster's seven-phase interview framework: requirements clarification, back-of-envelope estimation, API design, high-level design, database design, deep dives, and wrap-up.
5. The repository is useful as a read-later routing map, not as direct evidence for production engineering decisions; each linked article, paper, or case study needs its own source-specific credibility grade before claims harden into Tolaria fact.
