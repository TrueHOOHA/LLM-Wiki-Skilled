# How to walk through walls

## Summary
Henrik Karlsson's article argues that "hacker mindset" means refusing to treat official or socially taught abstractions as the whole system. The reusable mechanism is to ask what substrate actually determines outcomes: video-game memory and collision rules rather than villages and walls, production constraints rather than film-school roles, people and files rather than bureaucracy, or problems and trust rather than job postings. Tolaria should preserve this as [[Hacker Mindset]] and [[Abstraction-leak Reasoning]], not as inspirational advice or proof that every constraint is fake. The evidence is medium as practitioner synthesis: the article supplies concrete qualitative cases and links to Gwern, VaccinateCA, Alice Maz, speedrunning, and Patrick McKenzie-style examples, but most claims are illustrative rather than empirically tested.

## Key Claims
1. Many systems present a convenient surface ontology, but action often depends on a lower-level substrate that leaks through the abstraction.
2. Speedrunners, Rodriguez, bureaucracy navigators, and some career-builders look strange from the surface game because they act on the underlying mechanics.
3. Developing this mindset usually requires hands-on contact with the technical or operational substrate, not only learning conventional roles, credentials, or procedures.
4. The useful pattern is diagnostic and constructive: identify false walls, test substrate-level affordances, and exploit legitimate degrees of freedom.
5. The unsafe version becomes manipulation or antisocial bypassing; ethics, legality, and safety constraints remain real even when surface process constraints are negotiable.

## Notable Quotes
- "Most systems can be viewed at multiple levels. There is a superficial system which pretends to be made of one thing... But actually, it is really made of something else..."
- "If you learn to understand that underlying system, you can find ways to use the lower-level details to steer the system in a way that looks incomprehensible to those who only see the more superficial system."
- "A bureaucracy is just people and some file systems."
- "If you are someone who is already creative, and then you become technical, then you are unstoppable."
- "If you lack ethics, hacker mindset can be used in manipulative and anti-social ways."
