# Podcast Notes tweet on anterior mid-cingulate cortex and will to live

## Summary
This weak social source claims that the anterior mid-cingulate cortex (aMCC) "only grows when you do things you don't want to do" and is "the same region associated with the will to live," using a short Huberman/Goggins video clip as the hook. The captured tweet provides no primary citations or transcript; the useful Tolaria payload is a skeptical claim-vs-evidence note, not a self-help prescription. Source checking supports a narrower [[Anterior Mid-cingulate Cortex and Tenacity]] framing: aMCC is implicated in effort, cost/benefit computation, attention allocation, goal pursuit, and perseverance-related subjective states, but the exact viral growth and "will to live" phrasing is overstated.

## Key Claims
1. The tweet claims the aMCC grows only when a person does unwanted or difficult tasks.
2. It claims the aMCC is associated with "the will to live."
3. It frames David Goggins' reaction to Andrew Huberman as if the clip validates the claim.
4. The source-check found adjacent evidence for aMCC involvement in tenacity/perseverance but not for the exclusive growth claim.
5. PubMed searches for exact/near-exact "will to live" + anterior midcingulate phrasing did not surface a direct primary source during ingestion.

## Notable Quotes
- "Your brain has a region that only grows when you do things you don't want to do."
- "It's called the anterior mid-cingulate cortex."
- "It's also the same region associated with the will to live."
- "Watch David Goggins' face when Huberman tells him this for the first time."
