# Matt Gittleson on vibecoded B2C app exit playbook

## Summary
Matt Gittleson's X long-form article claims he built the B2C citation-checking app [[CiteSure]], grew it with founder-made short-form content, and sold it to [[Jenni AI]] for $375,000 within six months. The source is useful as a practitioner playbook for [[Distribution-first AI-assisted B2C App Building]] and [[Operator Taste Before Content Automation]], but it remains weak social evidence for the financial, ARR, acquisition, and content-system performance claims. Alpha's strongest corroboration is narrower: the current CiteSure homepage displays "CiteSure by Jenni AI," supporting a product-to-Jenni connection without independently verifying the claimed sale price, ARR path, or production workflow.

## Key Claims
1. The article argues that AI coding tools reduce the technical moat for simple B2C apps, so attention, distribution, and organic UGC become the harder moat.
2. It recommends choosing a viral content format before building the product, then building only the minimum app needed to satisfy that format.
3. It recommends Cursor / [[Claude Code]] over highly constrained no-code builders, with an MVP target of roughly two weeks after format selection.
4. It claims founders should personally make the first 100 videos before delegating or automating content production, because taste and viral-sense training are the asset.
5. It describes a production loop: 3 posts/day/account across TikTok/Reels/Shorts; 50 hook clips; max 3 uses per demo; re-filmed demos to avoid duplicate throttling; Sunday batch filming; and a 50/25/25 split across proven formats, iterations, and moonshots.
6. It claims CiteSure reached 30M views, peaked at $132k ARR, sold for $375k, and later grew under Jenni despite little or no new video output. These numeric claims are not independently verified in the captured source.
7. It warns that one-and-done viral apps are leaky buckets: retention/churn determine whether attention converts into durable acquisition value.
8. It frames strategic acquirers and inbound distribution exposure as preferable to generic marketplaces or broker-led exit processes.

## Notable Quotes
