# Query Fixture

## Question

**"What did Vannevar Bush propose in 'As We May Think', and how does it relate to modern knowledge management systems?"

## Expected Answer Structure

### Direct Answer

Vannevar Bush proposed the "Memex," a machine for storing, retrieving, and linking personal knowledge through associative trails. This directly anticipates modern hypertext, the web, and personal knowledge management (PKM) systems like Obsidian, Roam Research, and the LLM Wiki pattern itself.

### Supporting Evidence

- [[As We May Think]] — Bush described the Memex as a desk-like device with screens and a keyboard for navigating linked documents.
- [[The Memex]] — The Memex stored documents on microfilm and used associative trails to link them, anticipating hyperlinks.
- [[Associative Trails]] — Bush argued that the human mind works by association, not hierarchical classification, which is the foundation of modern linked note-taking.

### Gaps and Uncertainties

- Bush focused on microfilm storage; modern systems use digital databases.
- The social sharing of trails was mentioned but not fully explored.

### Synthesis

Bush's Memex can be seen as the conceptual ancestor of both the World Wide Web and modern PKM tools. The LLM Wiki pattern extends this by having an LLM maintain the associative structure automatically.
