# As We May Think

**Author**: Vannevar Bush
**Publication**: The Atlantic, July 1945
**URL**: https://www.theatlantic.com/magazine/archive/1945/07/as-we-may-think/303881/

## Summary

Vannevar Bush's seminal 1945 essay envisioning a machine called the "Memex" — a device for storing, retrieving, and linking personal knowledge. The Memex would allow users to create "trails" of associative links between documents, anticipating hypertext and the modern web.

## Key Claims

1. Information overload is a growing problem that requires new organizational tools.
2. The human mind works by association, not by strict hierarchical classification.
3. A machine could augment human memory by storing and linking documents.
4. "Trails" of associative links could be shared between users.

## Notable Quotes

> "The human mind [...] operates by association. With one item in its grasp, it snaps instantly to the next that is suggested by the association of thoughts, in accordance with some intricate web of trails carried by the cells of the brain."

## Entities Mentioned

- Vannevar Bush — Author of the essay, director of the Office of Scientific Research and Development during WWII.
- The Memex — A hypothetical electromechanical device for storing and retrieving information.

## Concepts Mentioned

- Associative trails — The idea that knowledge is organized by links between related ideas, not by alphabetical or hierarchical order.
- Hypertext — Though not named as such, the concept of linked documents.
